# Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita, 2023 (BNSS)

## Basic Information
- **Full Name**: Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (Indian Citizen Security Code)
- **Short Name**: BNSS
- **Year of Enactment**: 2023
- **Act Number**: Act No. 46 of 2023
- **Effective Date**: July 1, 2024
- **Status**: CURRENT LAW (Active)
- **Jurisdiction**: All of India
- **Type**: Procedural Criminal Law
- **Total Sections**: 531 sections
- **Chapters**: 38 chapters
- **Schedules**: 5 schedules
- **PDF Source**: https://www.indiacode.nic.in/bitstream/123456789/21920/1/the_bharatiya_nagarik_suraksha_sanhita%2c_2023.pdf

## Purpose and Scope
BNSS replaces the colonial-era Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) 1973 with a modernized procedural framework. It revolutionizes HOW criminal justice is administered in India through technology integration, faster timelines, and victim-centric approach.

## Why BNSS Was Enacted

### 1. Technology Integration
- Complete digitalization of criminal justice system
- E-FIR, e-filing, e-summons
- Video conferencing for trials
- Electronic evidence handling
- Digital signatures and authentication
- Online case tracking

### 2. Speed and Efficiency
- **Strict timelines** at every stage
- Charge sheet within 90 days (clear deadline)
- Judgment within 30-45 days of trial conclusion
- Fast-track courts provisions
- Reduced adjournments
- Time-bound justice delivery

### 3. Victim-Centric Approach
- Enhanced victim rights
- Mandatory victim impact statements
- Better compensation provisions
- Regular case status updates to victims
- Victim participation at critical stages
- Protection during proceedings

### 4. Modern Policing
- Mandatory forensic investigation for serious crimes
- Audio-visual recording of procedures
- Zero FIR (can file anywhere)
- Better investigation standards
- Scientific evidence emphasis

### 5. Decolonization
- Remove colonial mindset
- Indian terminology and approach
- Citizen-friendly procedures
- Accessibility and transparency

## Structure
- **38 Chapters** (one more than CrPC's 37)
- **531 Sections** (47 more than CrPC's 484)
- **5 Schedules** containing forms and classifications
- Reorganized for better logical flow

## Revolutionary Changes from CrPC

### 1. Zero FIR (Section 173)
**GAME-CHANGING PROVISION**
- FIR can be registered at ANY police station (not just jurisdictional)
- No police station can refuse FIR
- FIR must be transferred to jurisdictional station within 15 days
- Ensures no victim is turned away
- Major reform for accessible justice

### 2. E-FIR (Section 173)
- **Online FIR registration**
- Can file FIR electronically
- Digital signature valid
- 24/7 accessibility
- Reduces need to visit police station physically

### 3. Mandatory Forensic Investigation (Section 176)
- For offences with 7+ years punishment: **Forensic investigation mandatory**
- Visit by forensic experts to crime scene
- Scientific evidence collection
- Improves investigation quality
- Reduces wrongful convictions

### 4. Audio-Visual Recording (Throughout BNSS)
- Recording of arrest (Section 43)
- Recording of statements (Section 183)
- Recording of confessions (Section 183)
- Recording of search and seizure
- Prevents manipulation and torture

### 5. Strict Timelines
- **Charge sheet: 90 days maximum** (Section 193)
- **Judgment: 30 days after arguments** (Section 399)
- **Trial completion: Time-bound**
- Default bail if timelines violated
- Accountability for delays

### 6. Video Conferencing (Multiple Sections)
- Bail hearings via video (Section 479-482)
- Evidence recording via video (Section 265)
- Witness examination remotely
- Reduces need for physical presence
- Convenient for all parties

### 7. Summary Trials Extended (Section 282-291)
- For offences with up to **3 years** punishment (CrPC had 2 years)
- Faster disposal of minor cases
- Simplified procedure
- Reduces case backlog

### 8. Community Service (Section 4)
- Alternative punishment option
- For petty offences
- Reformative justice
- Reduces prison burden

### 9. Victim Rights Enhanced
- **Victim impact statement mandatory** (Section 360)
- Regular case updates to victim
- Better compensation (Section 391)
- Victim can engage counsel
- Participation in proceedings

### 10. Organized Crime Provisions (Section 111)
- Specific procedures for organized crime
- Better tools to tackle syndicates
- Enhanced powers for investigation

## Key Chapters (Detailed)

### Chapter I: Preliminary (Sections 1-2)
- Title, extent, commencement
- Definitions (includes digital/electronic terms)

### Chapter II: Constitution of Criminal Courts (Sections 3-27)
**Court Hierarchy** (Same as CrPC but digitally enabled):
1. Supreme Court
2. High Courts
3. Courts of Session
4. Magistrates (CJM, JMFC I, JMFC II)

**Technology Integration**:
- Courts equipped with video conferencing
- Digital filing systems
- Electronic case management

### Chapter III: Powers of Courts (Sections 28-38)
- Sentencing powers unchanged from CrPC
- Sessions Court: Death, life, any term
- Magistrate First Class: Up to 3 years
- Magistrate Second Class: Up to 1 year

### Chapter IV: Jurisdiction of Courts (Sections 39-50)
- Territorial jurisdiction rules
- Special jurisdiction provisions
- Transfer of cases

### Chapter V: Powers of Arrest (Sections 35-60)
**CRITICAL - Enhanced Protections**

**Section 35**: Arrest without warrant
- Similar to CrPC 41 but with more safeguards
- Audio-visual recording mandatory
- Cannot arrest for offences with less than 3 years if conditions met

**Section 41**: Notice of appearance
- For offences with less than 7 years punishment
- Notice instead of arrest (similar to CrPC 41A)
- If person appears, no arrest

**Section 46**: Rights of arrested person (ENHANCED)
- **Must inform** grounds of arrest
- Right to bail (if bailable)
- Right to consult lawyer **immediately**
- **Inform family/friend within 12 hours** (CrPC was 8-12 hours)
- Right to medical examination
- Audio-visual recording of arrest memo

**Section 50**: Medical examination
- At request of arrested person
- To record pre-existing injuries
- Prevents false torture allegations

**Section 54**: 24-hour rule
- Must produce before magistrate within 24 hours
- Excludes journey time

**Important**: Audio-visual recording of arrest makes police accountable

### Chapter VI: Processes to Compel Appearance (Sections 61-90)
**Electronic Summons and Warrants**

**Section 205**: Summons via electronic means
- Email, SMS, WhatsApp
- Digital service of summons
- Faster and more efficient

**Section 73-82**: Warrants
**Section 83-85**: Proclamation and attachment (for absconding accused)

### Chapter VII-XI: Public Order and Security (Sections 129-172)
- Similar to CrPC provisions
- Section 163: Prohibitory orders (like CrPC 144)
- Maintenance (Section 144-147) - similar to CrPC 125

### Chapter XII: Information to Police and Investigation (Sections 173-193)
**MOST TRANSFORMED CHAPTER**

**Section 173: FIR (Zero FIR + E-FIR)**
- **Zero FIR**: Register at ANY police station
- **E-FIR**: Online registration possible
- Free copy to informant (physical or electronic)
- Transfer to jurisdictional station within 15 days
- No police station can refuse

**Section 174**: Information about non-cognizable offence
- Cannot investigate without magistrate order

**Section 176: Police power to investigate + MANDATORY FORENSICS**
- Police can investigate cognizable offence
- **MANDATORY forensic investigation for offences with 7+ years punishment**
- Forensic team visit to scene
- Scientific evidence collection
- Major improvement in investigation quality

**Section 180: Examination of witnesses**
- Similar to CrPC 161
- Cannot be on oath
- Statement during investigation

**Section 183: Recording of confessions and statements**
- Magistrate can record
- **Audio-visual recording mandatory**
- Must be voluntary
- Major safeguard against coercion

**Section 184: Search and seizure**
- Similar powers as CrPC
- Audio-visual recording encouraged

**Section 187: Remand (Critical Section)**
- **Like CrPC 167 but with strict timelines**
- Accused produced within 24 hours
- Police custody maximum 15 days at a time
- Total custody limits:
  - **60 days** for offences with up to 10 years
  - **90 days** for death/life/10+ years offences
- **After 60/90 days: Default bail** if charge sheet not filed
- **Video conferencing for remand hearings allowed**

**Section 193: Charge Sheet (90-Day Deadline)**
- **Police MUST file charge sheet within 90 days**
- Clear timeline (CrPC was ambiguous)
- If not filed within 90 days: Default bail
- Strict accountability
- Major reform for speedy justice

### Chapter XIII: Jurisdiction (Sections 194-206)
- Where offences may be tried

### Chapter XIV: Conditions for Initiation of Proceedings (Sections 207-223)
**Taking Cognizance**

**Section 210: Cognizance by magistrate**
- Similar to CrPC 190
- Can take cognizance from:
  1. Complaint
  2. Police report (charge sheet)
  3. Information from any person
  4. Own knowledge
- **Electronic filing accepted**

**Section 218**: Sanction for prosecution of public servants
- Similar to CrPC 197
- Prior government sanction needed

### Chapter XV-XVII: Trial Procedures (Sections 224-291)
**Enhanced with Technology**

**Section 256-265**: Trial before Sessions Court
- **Section 265: Video conferencing for evidence**
- Witnesses can testify via video link
- Remote testimony allowed
- Reduces travel, delays

**Section 266-281**: Trial before Magistrates (Warrant Cases)
**Section 282-291**: Summary Trials
- **Extended to 3 years** (was 2 years in CrPC)
- Fast-track disposal
- Simplified procedure

### Chapter XVIII: Judgment (Sections 399-401)
**REVOLUTIONARY TIMELINE**

**Section 399: Judgment within 30 days**
- **After conclusion of arguments, judgment MUST be delivered within 30 days**
- Can extend by 15 days with reasons (total 45 days max)
- Strict timeline to prevent indefinite delays
- Accountability of judges
- Major reform for speedy justice

**Section 400**: Copy of judgment
- Electronic copy to accused
- Digital delivery

**Section 401**: Judgment in open court
- Must be delivered publicly
- Can't be in chambers

### Chapter XIX-XXV: Various Procedures (Sections 292-378)
- Appeals, revisions, references
- Similar structure as CrPC but digitalized

### Chapter XXVI: Bail and Bonds (Sections 479-490)
**Technology-Enabled Bail**

**Section 479: Bail in bailable offences**
- Similar to CrPC 436
- Right to bail
- Electronic bail bonds accepted

**Section 480: Bail in non-bailable offences**
- Similar to CrPC 437
- Court discretion
- Factors: nature of offence, evidence, likelihood of absconding
- **Video conferencing for bail hearings**

**Section 482: Anticipatory bail**
- Similar to CrPC 438
- Pre-arrest bail
- Sessions Court or High Court
- **Can be filed electronically**
- **Video hearing possible**

**Section 483**: Powers of High Court/Sessions Court for bail
- Similar to CrPC 439

**Electronic Bonds**: All bail bonds can be electronic with digital signatures

### Chapter XXVII: Victim Rights and Compensation (Sections 357-392)
**MAJOR VICTIM-CENTRIC REFORMS**

**Section 360: Victim Impact Statement (NEW)**
- **Mandatory victim impact statement** before sentencing
- Victim can tell how crime affected them
- Court considers impact in sentencing
- Major reform for victim participation

**Section 391: Compensation to victims (ENHANCED)**
- Court can award compensation from fine
- **State must compensate victims**
- For physical, mental, financial loss
- Interim compensation during trial
- Rehabilitation support
- **Better than CrPC 357**

**Section 392**: Victim assistance
- State to provide assistance
- Legal aid, counseling, support services

**Victim Updates**: Throughout BNSS, provisions for regular case status updates to victims

### Chapter XXVIII: Appeals (Sections 408-436)
**Electronic Filing**

**Section 410**: Appeal from conviction
- Similar to CrPC 374
- 30 days to file
- **Electronic filing accepted**
- **Video hearings possible**

**Section 413**: Appeal by state
- Against acquittal
- Against inadequate sentence

### Chapter XXIX: Reference and Revision (Sections 437-447)
**Section 443**: High Court revisional powers
- Similar to CrPC 397-401
- Can call for records
- Can examine correctness

### Chapter XXX: Transfer (Sections 448-454)
**Section 449**: Transfer of cases
- For fair trial
- By High Court or Supreme Court

### Chapter XXXI: Execution of Sentences (Sections 455-478)
- How sentences executed
- Suspension, remission

### Chapter XXXII-XXXVIII: Various Provisions (Sections 491-531)
- Property disposal
- Preventive actions
- Irregularities
- Miscellaneous

## Strict Timelines (Major Reform)

### Investigation Stage
- **Forensic team**: Must visit scene within 24-48 hours (for serious crimes)
- **Remand**: Cannot exceed 60/90 days
- **Charge sheet: 90 days maximum** (Section 193)

### Trial Stage
- **Cognizance**: Promptly after charge sheet
- **Charge framing**: Within reasonable time
- **Trial**: Time-bound proceedings
- **Judgment: 30 days after arguments** (Section 399)

### Appeal Stage
- **Filing**: 30 days from conviction
- **Hearing**: Time-bound
- **Decision**: Within reasonable period

**Default Bail**: If timelines violated (90 days charge sheet deadline), accused gets bail automatically

## Forensic Investigation (Section 176)

### When Mandatory
- Offences punishable with **7 years or more**
- Examples: Murder, rape, robbery, serious hurt, etc.

### What It Includes
- Crime scene visit by forensic experts
- Scientific evidence collection
- Photography, videography
- Fingerprint analysis
- DNA collection (if needed)
- Ballistics (if firearms involved)
- Digital forensics (for cyber crimes)

### Benefits
- Better quality investigation
- Reduces wrongful convictions
- Scientific approach
- Courts rely on forensic evidence

### Infrastructure
- Every district to have forensic facility
- Mobile forensic vans
- Trained forensic personnel
- State-of-art laboratories

## Transition from CrPC to BNSS

### Critical Date: July 1, 2024

**Before July 1, 2024**:
- All procedures under CrPC
- CrPC sections apply
- Old system

**On/After July 1, 2024**:
- All NEW procedures under BNSS
- BNSS sections apply
- New system

### For Ongoing Cases
- **FIR filed before July 1**: CrPC continues
- **FIR filed after July 1**: BNSS applies
- **Pending investigations**: Complete under old law
- **Trials started**: Continue under CrPC
- **New arrests**: BNSS procedures
