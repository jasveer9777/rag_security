# Indian Penal Code, 1860 (IPC)

## Basic Information
- **Full Name**: Indian Penal Code
- **Year of Enactment**: 1860
- **Effective Date**: January 1, 1862
- **Status**: REPLACED by Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023 on July 1, 2024
- **Jurisdiction**: All of India
- **Type**: Substantive Criminal Law
- **Total Sections**: 511 sections (originally)
- **PDF Source**: https://www.indiacode.nic.in/bitstream/123456789/15289/1/ipc_act.pdf

## Purpose and Scope
The Indian Penal Code defines criminal offences and prescribes punishments for crimes committed in India. It is the primary criminal code of India covering all substantive aspects of criminal law.

## Structure
- **23 Chapters** organized by type of offence
- Covers offences from murder to defamation to property crimes
- Defines mens rea (mental element) and actus reus (physical act) for crimes

## Key Chapters
1. **Chapter I**: Introduction (Sections 1-5)
2. **Chapter II**: General Explanations (Sections 6-52A)
3. **Chapter III**: Punishments (Sections 53-75)
4. **Chapter IV**: General Exceptions (Sections 76-106)
5. **Chapter V**: Abetment (Sections 107-120)
6. **Chapter VI**: Offences Against the State (Sections 121-130)
7. **Chapter XVI**: Offences Affecting Human Body (Sections 299-377)
   - Murder (302), Culpable Homicide (299-304)
   - Hurt and Grievous Hurt (319-338)
   - Assault (351-358)
8. **Chapter XVII**: Offences Against Property (Sections 378-462)
   - Theft (378-382)
   - Robbery (390-402)
   - Cheating (415-420)
9. **Chapter X**: Contempt of Lawful Authority (Sections 172-190)
10. **Chapter XXI**: Defamation (Sections 499-502)

## Punishments Defined
- Death Penalty
- Life Imprisonment
- Imprisonment (Rigorous or Simple)
- Forfeiture of Property
- Fine

## Related Laws
- **Replaced By**: Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023
- **Works With**: Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) - for procedure
- **Works With**: Indian Evidence Act - for proving crimes
- **Special Laws**: Many special acts override IPC provisions

## Historical Context
- Drafted by Lord Macaulay in 1860
- Based on English Common Law
- One of the first codifications of criminal law in the world
- Remained largely unchanged for 160+ years until BNS replaced it
